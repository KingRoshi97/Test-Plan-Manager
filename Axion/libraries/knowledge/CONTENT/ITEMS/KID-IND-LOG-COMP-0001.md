# Audit + Retention Expectations (Logistics)

## Summary
This checklist outlines actionable steps for ensuring compliance with audit and retention requirements in logistics and supply chain operations. It focuses on maintaining accurate records, adhering to regulatory standards, and implementing best practices for data retention and accessibility.

## When to Use
- During internal or external audits of logistics operations.
- When establishing or reviewing data retention policies for supply chain records.
- To ensure compliance with industry standards and legal requirements for logistics documentation.
- When onboarding new systems or processes that impact record-keeping.

## Do / Don't

### Do:
1. **Document retention policies**: Define clear retention periods for all logistics records based on regulatory and business requirements.
2. **Implement secure storage solutions**: Use encrypted databases or cloud storage systems to protect sensitive supply chain data.
3. **Conduct regular audits**: Schedule periodic reviews of logistics records to ensure compliance with retention policies and data accuracy.
4. **Train staff**: Provide training to employees on audit and retention requirements to ensure consistent practices.
5. **Maintain audit trails**: Ensure all transactions and changes are logged with timestamps and user identification for traceability.

### Don't:
1. **Ignore regulatory requirements**: Avoid assuming retention periods without referencing applicable laws and standards.
2. **Store data indefinitely**: Do not retain records longer than necessary, as this increases security risks and storage costs.
3. **Use unsecured storage**: Avoid using non-compliant or unencrypted systems for sensitive logistics data.
4. **Neglect data classification**: Do not treat all records equally; classify data based on sensitivity and retention requirements.
5. **Delay audits**: Avoid postponing scheduled audits, as this can lead to compliance gaps and operational risks.

## Core Content

### Audit Expectations
1. **Define audit scope**: Identify the specific records, processes, and systems subject to auditing. Include shipping manifests, inventory logs, supplier contracts, and billing records.
   - *Rationale*: A clearly defined scope ensures audits are focused and efficient, reducing the risk of missing critical compliance issues.
2. **Standardize record formats**: Use consistent templates and formats for logistics records to facilitate easier auditing.
   - *Rationale*: Standardization reduces errors and ensures auditors can quickly verify information.
3. **Prepare audit trails**: Ensure all system changes, transactions, and communications are logged with timestamps, user IDs, and relevant metadata.
   - *Rationale*: Audit trails are essential for verifying the integrity of records and tracing discrepancies.

### Retention Expectations
1. **Establish retention periods**: Define retention periods for logistics records based on legal, regulatory, and operational requirements (e.g., 7 years for financial records in many jurisdictions).
   - *Rationale*: Retention policies ensure compliance and prevent unnecessary storage costs.
2. **Classify records**: Categorize records into groups (e.g., operational, financial, compliance-related) and assign retention periods accordingly.
   - *Rationale*: Classification prevents over-retention and ensures critical records are prioritized.
3. **Implement automated deletion**: Use software tools to enforce retention policies and automatically delete records that exceed their retention period.
   - *Rationale*: Automation reduces human error and ensures consistent compliance.
4. **Secure archived data**: Encrypt archived records and restrict access to authorized personnel only.
   - *Rationale*: Protecting archived data minimizes security risks and ensures compliance with data protection regulations.

### Compliance and Monitoring
1. **Audit compliance regularly**: Conduct annual or semi-annual audits to ensure retention policies are followed and records are accurate.
2. **Update policies**: Review and update retention and audit policies annually or whenever regulations change.
3. **Monitor access logs**: Regularly review access logs to detect unauthorized access or suspicious activity.
