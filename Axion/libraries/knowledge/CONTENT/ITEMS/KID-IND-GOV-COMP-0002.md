# Retention + Records Management Overview

## Summary
Retention and records management are critical components of government operations, ensuring compliance with legal mandates, safeguarding sensitive information, and preserving institutional memory. This checklist provides actionable steps for implementing effective retention and records management practices in public sector organizations, emphasizing compliance, security, and operational efficiency.

## When to Use
- When establishing or updating a records management policy for government agencies.
- During audits or compliance reviews to ensure adherence to legal and regulatory requirements.
- When implementing new systems or migrating records to digital platforms.
- When handling records related to sensitive or classified information.
- To prepare for Freedom of Information Act (FOIA) requests or other public disclosure obligations.

## Do / Don't

### Do:
1. **Do classify records by type and sensitivity**: Use a taxonomy that aligns with legal and organizational requirements.
2. **Do establish retention schedules**: Define how long each type of record must be retained based on its purpose and legal mandates.
3. **Do implement secure disposal methods**: Ensure records are destroyed in a manner that prevents unauthorized access or data breaches.

### Don't:
1. **Don't retain records indefinitely without justification**: This increases storage costs and legal risks.
2. **Don't mix personal and official records**: Maintain clear boundaries to avoid compliance issues.
3. **Don't ignore metadata**: Metadata is critical for tracking the lifecycle of records and ensuring proper management.

## Core Content

### 1. **Establish a Records Management Policy**
   - Define the scope of records management, including physical and digital records.
   - Identify roles and responsibilities (e.g., records officers, IT staff, compliance officers).
   - Ensure the policy aligns with applicable laws such as the Federal Records Act, state-specific mandates, and industry standards like ISO 15489.

   **Rationale**: A clear policy provides a foundation for consistent, compliant practices.

### 2. **Classify and Inventory Records**
   - Conduct a records inventory to identify all records, their formats, and storage locations.
   - Categorize records by type (e.g., financial, personnel, legal) and sensitivity (e.g., classified, public, restricted).
   - Use a standardized taxonomy to ensure consistency across departments.

   **Rationale**: Classification enables efficient retrieval, compliance with retention schedules, and appropriate security measures.

### 3. **Define Retention Schedules**
   - Consult legal and regulatory requirements to determine minimum retention periods.
   - Establish retention schedules for each record type, specifying retention periods and disposal methods.
   - Regularly review and update schedules to reflect changes in laws or organizational needs.

   **Rationale**: Retention schedules reduce legal risks and ensure records are retained only as long as necessary.

### 4. **Implement Secure Storage Solutions**
   - Use secure physical storage for paper records, such as locked cabinets or restricted-access archives.
   - For digital records, implement encryption, access controls, and regular backups.
   - Ensure storage solutions comply with standards like NIST SP 800-53 for cybersecurity.

   **Rationale**: Secure storage protects sensitive information from unauthorized access or loss.

### 5. **Establish Disposal Procedures**
   - Use shredding, degaussing, or other secure methods for physical and digital records disposal.
   - Maintain a disposal log to document destroyed records, including dates and methods.
   - Ensure disposal methods comply with laws such as the Privacy Act and HIPAA (if applicable).

   **Rationale**: Proper disposal minimizes the risk of data breaches and unauthorized access.

### 6. **Train Staff and Monitor Compliance**
   - Provide regular training on records management policies and procedures.
   - Use audits and monitoring tools to ensure compliance with retention schedules and security protocols.
   - Address non-compliance promptly through corrective actions.

   **Rationale**: Training and monitoring ensure consistent application of policies and mitigate risks.
