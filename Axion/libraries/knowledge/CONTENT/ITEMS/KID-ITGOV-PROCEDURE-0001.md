# Compliance Change Procedure (Policy Update)

## Summary
This procedure outlines the steps required to implement a compliance-related policy update within the platform operations environment. It ensures all changes adhere to regulatory requirements, maintain system integrity, and are auditable for compliance governance. Following this procedure minimizes risks associated with policy misalignment or operational disruptions.

## When to Use
- When a regulatory body mandates a new compliance requirement.
- When internal audits identify gaps in existing compliance policies.
- When updating policies to align with new industry standards or organizational goals.
- When preparing for external compliance certifications or renewals.

## Do / Don't

### Do:
1. **Do** validate the regulatory or organizational requirement driving the policy update.
2. **Do** involve all relevant stakeholders, including compliance officers, platform operations leads, and legal teams.
3. **Do** test the updated policy in a controlled environment before full deployment.

### Don't:
1. **Don't** bypass the approval process for urgent updates without proper documentation.
2. **Don't** implement changes without informing affected teams and providing training if necessary.
3. **Don't** proceed without ensuring the update is auditable and traceable.

## Core Content

### Prerequisites
1. Access to the compliance governance documentation and regulatory requirements.
2. Approval from the Compliance Governance Committee or equivalent authority.
3. A sandbox or staging environment to test policy updates without impacting production systems.
4. A rollback plan in case the update introduces unexpected issues.

### Procedure
1. **Identify the Requirement**
   - Review the regulatory or organizational change driving the update.
   - Document the specific compliance gaps or risks addressed by the policy change.
   - Expected Outcome: A clear understanding of the compliance requirement and its impact.
   - Common Failure Modes: Misinterpreting the requirement or failing to document it properly.

2. **Draft the Policy Update**
   - Collaborate with compliance officers and platform operations teams to draft the updated policy.
   - Ensure the draft includes measurable metrics to evaluate compliance.
   - Expected Outcome: A draft policy that aligns with regulatory and operational needs.
   - Common Failure Modes: Drafting ambiguous or non-actionable policies.

3. **Review and Approve**
   - Submit the draft to the Compliance Governance Committee for review.
   - Address feedback and obtain formal approval.
   - Expected Outcome: An approved policy ready for implementation.
   - Common Failure Modes: Delays in obtaining approval due to incomplete documentation.

4. **Test the Policy Update**
   - Deploy the updated policy in a sandbox or staging environment.
   - Conduct tests to ensure the policy works as intended and does not disrupt operations.
   - Expected Outcome: Validation of the policy’s effectiveness in a controlled environment.
   - Common Failure Modes: Insufficient testing leading to undetected issues.

5. **Implement the Policy Update**
   - Deploy the approved policy update to the production environment.
   - Notify all affected teams and provide training if necessary.
   - Expected Outcome: A successfully implemented policy update with minimal disruption.
   - Common Failure Modes: Poor communication leading to operational confusion.

6. **Monitor and Audit**
   - Monitor the system to ensure compliance with the updated policy.
   - Conduct periodic audits to validate adherence and identify areas for improvement.
   - Expected Outcome: Continuous compliance and operational alignment.
   - Common Failure Modes: Neglecting ongoing monitoring, leading to non-compliance over time.

### Post-Implementation
- Document the entire process for audit purposes.
- Archive the previous policy version for historical reference.
