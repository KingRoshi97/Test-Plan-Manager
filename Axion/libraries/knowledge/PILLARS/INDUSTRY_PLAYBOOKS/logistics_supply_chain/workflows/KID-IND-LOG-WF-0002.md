# Receiving + Putaway Workflow Map

# Receiving + Putaway Workflow Map

## Summary
The Receiving + Putaway Workflow Map is a logistical pattern designed to streamline the process of receiving goods into a warehouse and efficiently storing them in designated locations. It addresses common challenges in supply chain operations, such as delays, inventory inaccuracies, and inefficient space utilization. By implementing this workflow, organizations can improve operational efficiency, reduce errors, and optimize storage capacity.

---

## When to Use
- When your warehouse or distribution center handles high volumes of incoming goods and requires a scalable, repeatable process for receiving and storing inventory.
- When inventory inaccuracies or delays in putaway are causing disruptions in downstream processes like picking or shipping.
- When transitioning from manual or ad-hoc receiving processes to an automated or semi-automated workflow.
- When implementing a Warehouse Management System (WMS) that supports receiving and putaway functionality.

---

## Do / Don't

### Do:
1. **Use barcode scanning or RFID technology** to ensure accurate inventory tracking during receiving and putaway.
2. **Designate staging areas** for incoming goods to separate receiving from storage operations.
3. **Integrate your WMS** with transportation management systems (TMS) to pre-validate shipments before arrival.
4. **Train staff** on workflow steps and system usage to minimize errors and delays.
5. **Conduct regular audits** of inventory and workflow efficiency to identify bottlenecks.

### Don’t:
1. **Skip quality checks** during receiving; this can lead to defective or incorrect items entering inventory.
2. **Overload staging areas**; this creates congestion and delays in putaway operations.
3. **Ignore inventory discrepancies**; failure to reconcile errors during receiving leads to downstream issues.
4. **Neglect safety protocols** for handling heavy or hazardous goods during receiving and storage.
5. **Rely solely on manual processes** for high-volume operations; this increases the risk of human error.

## Core Content

### Problem
In logistics and supply chain operations, receiving and putaway are critical processes that directly impact inventory accuracy, warehouse efficiency, and customer satisfaction. Without a structured workflow, organizations face challenges such as misplaced inventory, delayed processing, and inefficient use of warehouse space.

### Solution Approach
The Receiving + Putaway Workflow Map provides a structured framework for handling incoming goods and storing them efficiently. It leverages technology, predefined processes, and clear roles to ensure smooth operations.

### Implementation Steps

#### 1. **Pre-Receiving Preparation**
   - Validate incoming shipments against purchase orders or advance shipping notices (ASNs) using your WMS.
   - Assign staging areas based on shipment type, size, or priority.
   - Ensure staff availability and equipment readiness (e.g., forklifts, pallet jacks, scanners).

#### 2. **Receiving Process**
   - Scan incoming goods using barcode or RFID readers to capture SKU, quantity, and condition.
   - Perform quality checks to confirm goods meet specifications (e.g., no damage, correct items).
   - Update inventory records in the WMS to reflect received goods.

#### 3. **Staging**
   - Temporarily store goods in staging areas to separate receiving from putaway operations.
   - Group items by storage type (e.g., bulk storage, high-turnover zones) for efficient putaway.

#### 4. **Putaway Process**
   - Use system-directed putaway functionality in your WMS to assign optimal storage locations based on inventory type, size, and turnover rates.
   - Transport goods to designated locations using forklifts or automated guided vehicles (AGVs).
   - Confirm putaway completion by scanning storage location and updating the WMS.

#### 5. **Post-Processing**
   - Reconcile inventory discrepancies identified during receiving or putaway.
   - Generate reports on receiving and putaway performance metrics (e.g., cycle time, accuracy).
   - Conduct regular reviews to optimize workflow efficiency and address bottlenecks.

### Tradeoffs
- **Automation vs. Manual Processes**: Automation reduces errors and speeds up operations but requires upfront investment in technology and training.
- **Standardized vs. Flexible Workflows**: Standardized workflows improve consistency but may not accommodate unique inventory types or warehouse layouts.
- **Staging Area Size**: Larger staging areas reduce congestion but may consume valuable warehouse space.

### Alternatives
- For small-scale operations, consider manual receiving and putaway processes with basic inventory tracking tools.
- For high-volume operations, explore fully automated solutions such as robotic putaway systems or conveyor-based workflows.
