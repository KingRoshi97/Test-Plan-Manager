# Conversion Funnel KPIs

# Conversion Funnel KPIs

## Summary  
Conversion Funnel KPIs are metrics used to measure the effectiveness of an eCommerce website in guiding users through the purchasing process. These KPIs track user behavior across stages such as awareness, consideration, decision, and purchase, providing actionable insights to optimize conversion rates and revenue.

---

## When to Use  
- Analyzing user behavior to identify bottlenecks in the purchasing process.  
- Evaluating the performance of marketing campaigns or landing pages.  
- Optimizing the customer journey for higher sales or lead generation.  
- Benchmarking against industry standards to improve competitive positioning.  

---

## Do / Don't  

### Do  
1. **Track KPIs across all funnel stages**: Ensure metrics are measured for awareness (e.g., impressions), consideration (e.g., product views), decision (e.g., cart additions), and purchase (e.g., completed transactions).  
2. **Segment KPIs by audience**: Use demographic, geographic, or behavioral segmentation to identify trends and personalize strategies.  
3. **Use A/B testing**: Experiment with changes to improve conversion rates, such as adjusting page layouts or call-to-action buttons.  

### Don't  
1. **Ignore drop-off rates**: Failing to analyze where users abandon the funnel can lead to missed opportunities for optimization.  
2. **Overlook mobile performance**: Neglecting mobile-specific KPIs can skew results, especially as mobile eCommerce continues to grow.  
3. **Rely solely on averages**: Aggregate data can hide critical insights; always analyze granular metrics alongside averages.  

---

## Core Content  

### Key Definitions  
1. **Conversion Rate**: Percentage of users who complete a desired action (e.g., purchase, sign-up). Formula: `(Conversions ÷ Total Visitors) × 100`.  
2. **Cart Abandonment Rate**: Percentage of users who add items to their cart but do not complete the purchase. Formula: `(Abandoned Carts ÷ Total Carts Created) × 100`.  
3. **Bounce Rate**: Percentage of visitors who leave the site after viewing only one page. Formula: `(Single Page Visits ÷ Total Visits) × 100`.  

### Parameters to Track  
| **Stage**        | **KPI Examples**                 | **Purpose**                               |  
|-------------------|----------------------------------|-------------------------------------------|  
| Awareness         | Impressions, Click-Through Rate | Assess reach and engagement of campaigns. |  
| Consideration     | Product Views, Time on Page     | Evaluate interest in specific products.   |  
| Decision          | Cart Additions, Checkout Starts | Measure intent to purchase.               |  
| Purchase          | Completed Transactions, Revenue | Track final conversions and sales impact. |  

### Configuration Options  
1. **Tracking Tools**: Use platforms like Google Analytics, Adobe Analytics, or Shopify Analytics to collect and analyze funnel data.  
2. **Custom Events**: Configure events such as "Add to Cart" or "Checkout Start" to monitor specific user actions.  
3. **Attribution Models**: Select appropriate attribution models (e.g., last-click, linear) to understand the impact of marketing channels on conversions.  

### Lookup Values  
- **Industry Benchmarks**:  
  - Average eCommerce Conversion Rate: 2-3%.  
  - Cart Abandonment Rate: 60-80%.  
  - Bounce Rate: 20-45% for eCommerce websites.  
- **Segmentation Criteria**:  
  - Device Type (Mobile, Desktop, Tablet).  
  - Traffic Source (Organic, Paid, Referral).  
  - User Demographics (Age, Gender, Location).
