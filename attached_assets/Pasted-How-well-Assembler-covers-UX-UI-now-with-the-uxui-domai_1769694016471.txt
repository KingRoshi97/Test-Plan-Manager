⚠️ Gaps (what still needs 1 more layer to hit “premium” consistently)

Even with all this, agents still tend to drift unless you add three specific things:

Per-screen “states contract”

For every screen: explicit loading/empty/error/success requirements, exact copy, and what components render.

You have a lot of this, but make it mandatory + standardized so it’s not optional prose.

Component inventory + variants

A single table listing each component, variants, and where it’s allowed.

Example: StatusBadge variants and which states map to which variant.

This prevents style mismatch across pages.

Brand tokens wired to your real palette

Right now tokens are placeholders (UNKNOWN). To match your logo scheme, you should lock:

accent gradient stops

neutrals (dark surfaces + borders)

status colors

focus ring color

Otherwise the agent will pick random oranges/reds and your “Axiom” look won’t be consistent.

Your logo/color scheme impact

Your logo reads as: black base + warm amber/gold type + orange→red gradient mark. That’s a great premium direction, but it’s easy to overuse. Your design system should explicitly state:

gradient usage: primary CTA only + small highlights

solid accent: amber/gold for focus + active states

neutrals: deep charcoal surfaces (not pure black everywhere)
